About us

OUR PURPOSE

The Cabanga Foundation, a Non-Profit Company, was established in 2016 by Charne Permall (Industrial Psychologist) and Laetitia Permall (Educational Psychologist) as a social change initiative to facilitate youth and women development and empowerment in South Africa. The founders, Charne Permall and Laetitia Permall, a mother and daughter duo, have always had a passion to develop youth and work with them and what better way than to come together to change the lives of our young people to build our future. We have always wanted to make a difference and give back in a way that can impact the lives of others.

UNDERSTANDING THE CONTEXT

The word cabanga means to “conceive” or “imagine” in Zulu and SeSotho. The vision of Cabanga Foundation is to help women and youth co-create new possibilities for their lives. It aligns itself with the National Development Plan 2030 which references the need for growth and development by bridging the employability and social entrepreneurship gap. Cabanga strives to create a space for “individuals to live the lives to which they aspire”. It furthermore aligns with the National Youth Policy 2020, which emphasizes the opportunity for South Africa to focus on building “the capacity of young people to enable them to take charge of their own well-being by building their assets and realizing their full potential”. Cabanga strives to facilitate access for youth through learning and growth initiatives to enhance their holistic development and well-being to become agents of positive social change, thereby impacting social and economic development in South Africa.

Objectives

The overall objective of the proposed project is to empower young men and women to develop and ignite a greater sense of self responsibiliity to co-create possibilities for their lives, fulfil their destiny and thrive.
The long-term objectives of the 4-day programme is to contribute towards:

● The personal empowerment of young men and women;
● The emergence of people to use the power of their purpose to create the life they want;
● The transformation of a positive sense of self in relation to others and the world;
● Positive social change.

Personal Mastery Process Outcomes

The benefits of engaging in this workshop process based on the Theory of Change are:

● Participants value themselves more and feel more empowered;
● Participants actively transform the way they relate to others in their daily lives;
● Participants’ personal, professional, and intimate relationships have greater authenticity, integrity and accountability;
● Participants confront and address dysfunctional and disempowering relations in their own lives, workplaces, and communities;
● Participants relate to themselves and others equally and with compassion.
● Participants have a community of like-minded individuals to support their dreams.
