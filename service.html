Capacity of Youth

Youth can be accessed within different social systems like schools, tertiary institutions, church and community organizations. Focus on youth is also part of South Africa’s National Youth Development Plan, 2020 and this alignment makes the niche area not only strategically focused but also has the potential for a greater societal impact. The policy emphasizes the need to “build the capacity of youth people to enable them to take charge of their own wellbeing by building their assests and realising their full potential”. The Cabanga Foundation can therefore make an enormous impact by assisting government to achieve this goal. The focus on youth as a network for change has the potential for greater impact by not only creating opportunities for development for youth at risk, but by tapping into youth as role models, which further optimizes youth as an invaluable resource for social change. The focus is to create an awareness amongst youth of the impact of their decision making on their future, as well as teaching them to take agency to access the internal and external resources available to them to facilitate and enhance their development. We furthermore aim to collaborate with tertiary institutions as strategic partners to provide learners with peer mentors as role models. The developmental years of students are unlocked as they start making choices with their subjects which directly impact their career choices.

Championing the cause

Today, there is a strong focus on the key areas within South Africa of programmes specialising in health, education, economic development, political rights, violence against women. A study conducted by Johns Hopkins University in the United States on the empowerment of women within developing countries have found that women who gain agency are two times more likely to experience high levels of hope for the future, three times for likely to report improved income, ten times more likely to have reported improved health and fifteen times for likely to have reported improved relationships. In line with the National Curriculum Framework, the following areas which are addressed in their Life Orientation Programme in Grade 10-12 through development of self in society and careers as well as building youth through the key focus area for strengthening youth service programmes and introduce new, community-based programmes to offer young people life-skills training, entrepreneurship training and opportunities to participate in community development programmes. Cabanga has a vision to create the possibility and sustainability for change within South Africa. The youth are our future, let’s grow the youth for the future!

OUR VISION
The vision of Cabanga Foundation is to help women and youth co-create new possibilities for their lives. It aligns itself with the National Development Plan 2030 which references the need for growth and development by bridging the employability and social entrepreneurship gap. Cabanga strives to create a space for “individuals to live the lives to which they aspire”. It furthermore aligns with the National Youth Policy 2020, which emphasizes the opportunity for South Africa to focus on building “the capacity of young people to enable them to take charge of their own well-being by building their assets and realizing their full potential”.
